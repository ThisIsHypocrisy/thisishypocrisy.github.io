Time Magazine's Manipulative Use Of A Photograph Of A Crying Girl
Aug 31, 2018
  • Tweet
  • Share

The Time magazine used a photograph of a crying Honduran girl at a border crossing in its July 2, 2018 magazine issue to symbolize Trump’s border immigration policy. The photograph of the girl was juxtaposed with Trump and the words “Welcome to America”. The goal is clear: the magazine is using the sad photograph to demonize Trump and the US government’s border immigration policy. Specifically, the magazine is protesting the policy of separating migrant families caught illegally crossing the border.

Unfortunately, there’s a serious problem for the magazine here. First, the girl was not separated from her family. She was with her mother when they encountered US border officials. The officials searched the mother on the spot and at no point in the encounter separated the girl and her mother.

It’s unclear whether the magazine intentionally misled their audience with the photograph or they made an erroneous assumption. In both cases, the implications are troublesome. The magazine was either manipulating facts to exploit the plight of the girl to push an agenda, or it was sloppy with its research, something that should not happen with a top tier publication.

This is another example of the corporate media pushing a narrative at the expense of facts.
