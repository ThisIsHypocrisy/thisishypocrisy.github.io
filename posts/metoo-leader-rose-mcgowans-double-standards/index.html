MeToo Leader Rose McGowan's Double Standards
Aug 25, 2018

Rose McGowan is one of the leaders of the MeToo movement. On November 17th, 2017, she tweeted:

It’s quite simple, all who have worked with known predators should do 3 simple things. 1) Believe survivors 2) Apologize for putting your careers and wallets before what was right 3) Grab a spine and denounce. If you do not do these things you are still moral cowards. #ROSEARMY

When a bombshell report revealed that Asia Argento agreed to pay $380,000 to an actor who accused her of sexually assaulting him when he was 17 years old, she tweet:

None of us know the truth of the situation and I’m sure more will be revealed. Be gentle,” she added, sparking condemnations and accusations of hypocrisy.

Her double standards are clear here. For her friends, they deserve to be innocent until proven guilty. For others, they are guilty until proven innocent. Beyond this incident, it’s crazy that she, and the rest of the MeToo movement, actually believes guilty until proven innocent is civil and moral.

McGowan has since deleted the tweet.
