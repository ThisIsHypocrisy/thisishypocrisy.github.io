The Media Misleads Public On Trump's Fish-Feeding Technique In A PR Event With The Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe
Aug 25, 2018
  • Tweet
  • Share

In November last year, a story came out that Donald Trump, while in a fish-feeding PR event with the Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe in the Akasaka Palace, was impatient and ignorant of foreign customs and dumped an entire box of fish food into the pond.

Here are a few examples:

  • “Trump and Abe spooning fish food into a pond. (Toward the end, @potus decided to just dump the whole box in for the fish)” - Justin Sink, Bloomberg’s White House reporter
  • “Big stupid baby dumps a load of fish food on Japanese koi pond” - Jezebel
  • “Trump and Japanese PM Shinzo Abe were scheduled to feed koi spoonfuls of food. Until Trump poured his entire box of fish food into the pond.” - CNBC’s Christina Wilkie (deleted tweet)
  • “White House reporters, keen perhaps to pick up on a Trump gaffe, captured the moment when he upended his box on their smartphones and tweeted evidence of his questionable grasp of fish keeping. Some speculated that a poor palace employee would be dispatched to the scene to clean up the mess as soon as the two leaders disappeared inside.” - The Guardian (this article was later corrected)
  • “President Trump feeds fish with PM Shinzo Abe in Japan, then pours the entire box of food into the koi pond.” - CNN’s Veronica Rocha

The problem is, Trump wasn’t the only one who poured his entire box of fish food into the pond. Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe actually did it first.

The scary thing is, CNN’s tweet actually contained a video that purposely edited out Abe so viewers would not see that Trump was actually following Abe’s lead.
