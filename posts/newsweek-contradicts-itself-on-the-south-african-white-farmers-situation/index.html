Newsweek Contradicts Itself on the South African White Farmers Situation
Aug 25, 2018
  • Tweet
  • Share

It’s widely known that the South African government is seizing land without fair compensation from the nation’s white-owned farms. In March 1st, 2018, Quartz Africa published this article, “Expropriating land without compensation is impossible - take it from Zimbabwe”. On August 21st, 2018, news.com.au released this article, “South Africa begins seizing white-owned farms”.

In response to this news, Trump tweeted:

I have asked Secretary of State @SecPompeo to closely study the South Africa land and farm seizures and expropriations and the large scale killing of farmers. “South African Government is now seizing land from white farmers.

The mainstreaming media immediately pounced on Trump’s tweet by claiming that this narrative is perpertrated by “white supremacists”. First of all, it’s unfortunate that the media loves to arbitrarily give hateful labels like “white supremacist” and “racist” to anyone they disagree with. Often times, there is no concrete proof or data that these people are actually white supremacists or racists and there is a huge cost to anyone being labeled as such. These labels should NOT be thrown around lightly.

One of the media organizations that responded to Trump’s tweet is Newsweek. They recently published an article titled “WHITE NATIONALISTS PRAISE DONALD TRUMP FOR SPREADING WHITE SOUTH AFRICAN FARMERS CONSPIRACY THEORY”. Unfortunately, it’s quite hypocritical of Newsweek to claim Donald Trump is spreading this “conspiracy theory” when they themsleves published an article on March 19th titled “A WHITE FARMER IS KILLED EVERY FIVE DAYS IN SOUTH AFRICA AND AUTHORITIES DO NOTHING ABOUT IT, ACTIVISTS SAY”
